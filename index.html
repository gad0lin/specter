The Concept

A mystery that lives
in physical space

LORE turns any real location into an immersive story world. Robots scan the space with AI vision, generate a unique narrative grounded in what they actually find, then each robot becomes a character. No script. No predetermined story. Every space is different.

📸
Phase 1 — SCAN

Robots sweep the physical space autonomously. NVIDIA NIM vision identifies objects, text, and interesting details. Tavily searches real-world context. The AI builds a complete understanding of where it is.

NVIDIA NIM · Tavily · Unitree G1
✨
Phase 2 — GENERATE

Llama-3.3-70B on Nebius GPU generates a complete mystery — title, victim, suspects, clues, and solution — entirely grounded in what was actually found in the space. No two mysteries are the same.

Nebius Token Factory · Llama-3.3-70B
🎭
Phase 3 — INHABIT

Each robot is assigned a character. Holmes stations himself at the bay window. The suspect lingers near the bar. They speak in character, remember the conversation, and whisper intel about visitors to each other.

MiniMax TTS · Robot Mesh
🔍
Phase 4 — PLAY

Visitors are Watson. They walk between robots, collect clues, and solve the mystery. The mirror world dashboard shows every robot's position, visitor profiles from NIM vision, and the emerging story in real time.

OpenClaw · Omniverse (planned)

The Bigger Vision

A physical-world RPG.
Robots are the NPCs.

LORE is the foundation for something much larger — a new kind of entertainment that lives in real spaces, with real robots, and adapts to every player differently.

🗺

3D Space = Game World

The scanned physical space becomes a persistent game map. Robots know where they are in it. Players navigate through zones like a real RPG dungeon — except it's the Ferry Building rooftop.

👥

Multi-Player Quests

Multiple players registered simultaneously. One is Watson, one is Inspector Lestrade, one plays a shady associate. Each gets different clues. They must collaborate to solve what no one person can alone.

🎭

Robots Only See Players

NIM vision recognizes player tokens (colored badges, glow bracelets). A hundred people could be in the room — robots activate only for registered players. Bystanders get deflected: "Nothing to see here."

🧠

Robots Remember You

Per-player conversation history. If Watson already talked to Holmes, Holmes knows it. If you revealed something to the Suspect, they remember and adjust their story. Full persistent identity across the robot mesh.

🤖

Fleet Scales Infinitely

3 robots = a mystery. 10 robots = an escape room. 50 robots = a theme park. The story generator scales character count to match the fleet. Unitree G1 humanoids become actual walking NPCs.

🏛

Any Space, Any Story

Museum → artifacts become characters. Hotel → guests discover a heist. Conference → sponsor booths become crime scenes. Shopping mall → treasure hunt. Every space generates its own unique narrative from what's actually there.

🏛 Museums 🎪 Theme Parks 🔐 Escape Rooms 🏨 Hotels 🛍 Retail 🏢 Corporate Events 🎭 Immersive Theater
